Challenge Call

Challenge and Scenario Description

EEG-based auditory attention decoding (AAD) challenge aims to identify the direction of the attended speaker in multi-speaker environments from EEG signals. Despite the great progress, one critical limitation of current AAD studies lies in the lack of generalization capability to unseen subjects and sessions. Prior work predominantly restricted to single-subject within single-session, issues such as latent trial-dependent confound remain underexplored and there is a lack of systematic investigation into model generalization due to the scarcity of multi-modal stimulus data.

To address these issues and promote robust AAD research, this EEG-AAD challenge revolves around two key problems in AAD: cross-subject and cross-session tasks in multi-scenario settings. This competition will provide participants with the first multi-modal audio-visual auditory attention dataset simulating real-world scenes, containing approximately 4,400 minutes of EEG signals from 40 subjects across two sessions. Participants are challenged to push the boundaries of current methods and propose innovative solutions to address out-of-distribution generalization and data drift in EEG signals. We hope this challenge will advance the development of generalizable models for practical applications like hearing aids.

Tracks Overview

Details about the Tracks to be completed

Track 1: Cross-subject

This track challenges participants to build AAD models capable of decoding auditory attention categories from EEG signals across unseen subjects in audio-visual environments. Participants are provided with data from 30 subjects for training and validation, and data from 10 additional subjects for testing. The goal is to build a generalizable cross-subject decoding model, calculate the decoding accuracy of each subject in the test set, and finally obtain the average decoding accuracy(%).

Track 2: Cross-session

This track requires participants to bulid AAD models capable of decoding auditory attention categories from EEG signals of scenarios that subjects have never seen before. Data from 30 subjects in two scenarios are provided, where the audio-visual scenario data from the same subject is used for training, and the audio-only scenario data is used for validation. For the testing phase, data from an additional 10 subjects is provided, following the same process as validation: models trained on the audio-visual scenario are tested on data from the audio-only scenario. The goal of participants is to build a generalizable single-subject cross-session decoding model, calculate the decoding accuracy of each subject in the test set, and finally obtain the average decoding accuracy(%).

Data Description

Explore the datasets and evaluation metrics for the challenge

We provide a multi-modal AAD (MM-AAD) dataset consisting of EEG data collected from 40 subjects in two settings: audio-only and audio-visual scenes. Each subject was instructed to focus on one of two competing voices from the 90° left or right for an average of 55 minutes, resulting in a total of approximately 73.3 hours of data. Participants will build models to analyze the spatial orientation (left/right) of attention from EEG signals.

Audio-visual Scene

Subjects watched videos while wearing EEG caps. They focused on specific speakers based on gender cues, with gaze fixed on the speaker. Includes 55 minutes of EEG data per subject across 40 subjects.

Audio-only Scene

Subjects focused on audio stimuli while fixating on a crosshair. Attention direction was cued (left/right). Includes 55 minutes of EEG data per subject with attention validation via post-trial questions.

Evaluation Metric

Understand how your results will be evaluated

CorrectTestSamples： The number of test samples in a subject that correctly identify the direction of auditory attention.

TotalTestSamples： The total number of test samples given by a subject.

Subs： The total number of test subjects given by each track.

    Rules & Regulations

    • Participants are allowed to submit predictions up to five times for each track, but only the most recent submission will be considered.
    • Rankings will be determined based on the highest performance achieved in each track.
    • Participants have full flexibility in partitioning the MM-AAD dataset for training and processing.
    • However, test data must only be used for generating predictions and cannot be utilized for training. All parameter tuning should be conducted exclusively on the training set.
    • The use of external data (both training data and/or pretrained models) is allowed, if all conditions are met:
      • The datasets, or pretrained models, are publicly and freely available.
      • Datasets/pretrained models must be available before the start of this challenge.
      • Datasets/model weights used to generate predictions must be cited in the final description and in the final paper.
      • Public pretrained models, trained on private data can be used only after the approval of the organizers.
    • To ensure fairness and integrity, all participants must strictly adhere to the organizers' guidelines and meet the deadlines set for each stage of the competition.
    • If exceptional circumstances prevent compliance with the schedule, participants must provide a formal explanation via email.
    • The organizers reserve the right to interpret the evaluation rules, adjust rankings, or disqualify teams that fail to comply.

    Rules for ranking

    • Participants may choose to submit entries for one or both tracks, and rankings will be based on their best performance in each track.
    • The top three teams in each track will receive a certificate and be invited to submit a paper.
    • Participants are encouraged to communicate with the organizers via email for any questions or clarifications.
